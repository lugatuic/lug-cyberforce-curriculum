# Welcome / Intro to CyberForce Event
Event Agenda & Planning Guide

**Last updated:** June 2026  
**Owner:** AJ  
**When:** Early September 2026  
**Duration:** ~1.5 hours  
**Where:** [Room TBA]  
**Audience:** Everyone (competitors, interested people, curious folks)

---

## 🎯 Purpose

Give people a sense of what CyberForce is + what blue team work looks like. Let veterans share their experience. Build excitement + confidence.

**Goal:** People leave feeling "I can do this" (or "I'm definitely interested now").

---

## 📋 Rough Agenda (90 minutes)

**Welcome**
- AJ: "Hey everyone, welcome! We're excited to be here."
- Quick housekeeping (bathroom, questions, etc.)

**"What is CyberForce?" (AJ)**
- Why we do this competition
- What it's like (overview, not too technical)
- "You defend a network against live attacks"

**"I Competed in CyberForce" (Michael)**
- Michael's experience (1–2 competitions)
- What he did (his role on the team)
- What surprised him (good + hard)
- One key takeaway ("The learning was incredible")

**"My CyberForce Story" (Sam & Tegan)**
- Sam shares: Different perspective (maybe more technical focus)
- Tegan shares: Another angle (maybe more team/morale focused)
- Keep it relatable (not just about being perfect)

**Q&A from Veterans**
- Audience asks: "What was the hardest part?"
- "Did you know everything before starting?"
- "How did you handle stress?"
- "Would you do it again?"
- Veterans answer honestly

**"What's Next?" (AJ)**
- Registration timeline (mid-July)
- Team formation (how we balance teams)
- Workshops + events (Sept–Nov)
- Info session (early July if people want to learn more)
- Resources (link to docs, 2024 anomalies, etc.)

**Informal Socializing**
- Snacks (if available)
- Veterans hang around for 1-on-1 questions
- "Which team should I join?" type conversations

**Wrap-up**
- "Thanks for coming, we're excited to have you"
- "If interested, react 🎯 on Discord"
- "Questions? Ask in #cyberforce-chat"

---

## 📝 Key Talking Points

**AJ's intro (5 min):**
- "CyberForce is a national competition where 100+ teams from universities compete"
- "You defend energy infrastructure against live attacks"
- "It's intense, challenging, and genuinely educational"
- "Whether you're here to compete or just curious, welcome!"

**Michael's story (15 min):**
- "I competed twice, first as a team member, then as a captain"
- "Here's what I did, how we prepped, what happened on competition day"
- "The hardest part was: [be honest, could be Windows, Open PLC, time pressure, etc.]"
- "The best part was: [learning, teamwork, adrenaline, accomplishment]"
- "I'd do it again in a heartbeat"

**Q&A themes:**
- Preparation (do you need to know everything? No, we learn together)
- Stress (yes it's stressful, but it's manageable, we support each other)
- Outcomes (even if you don't win, the learning is massive)
- Time (yes, Sept–Nov is busy, but worth it)

**AJ's closer (5 min):**
- "Here's how to get involved: React 🎯 to show interest"
- "Info session in early July if you want to learn more before deciding"
- "Registration in mid-July, we'll help form teams"
- "Questions? Ask anytime. We're excited to have you."

## 🔄 Adaptations

**If hybrid (in-person + Zoom):**
- Have someone manage chat (answer questions)
- Speakers on camera
- Can be harder, but doable

**If only Zoom (no in-person):**
- Shorter event (45 min is better than 90)
- More interactive (polls, breakout Q&A)
- Less effective than in-person (but still worth doing)

**If large group (50+ people):**
- Need bigger room
- May need microphone / projector
- More formal feel, but still conversational

**If small group (5–10 people):**
- Super casual, ask more questions
- Maybe skip formal speakers, just conversation
