# Mentor Matching & Guidelines

**Last updated:** June 2026  
**Owner:** AJ  
**For:** Admins, mentors, team captains

---

## Overview

A mentor is a CyberForce veteran who helps a team through prep season. They provide:
- Experience + perspective
- Answers to technical questions
- Morale support
- Accountability (gentle reminders if team goes dark)

**Not required.** But recommended for teams that want extra support.

---

## Finding Mentors

**Reach out to:**
- Past CyberForce competitors 
- Graduate students interested in mentoring
- Faculty advisors with security interest
- Alumni who want to stay involved

**Ask:**
"Would you be interested in mentoring a CyberForce team this year? It's ~1–2 hrs/week Sept–Nov, helping a team with questions + support."

---

## Mentor-to-Team Matching

**Match based on:**

1. **Team needs**
   - Team struggling with Linux? → Find a Linux mentor
   - Team has nobody with web experience? → Find someone who knows web
   - Team is all newbies? → Find an experienced mentor (any area)

2. **Mentor expertise**
   - Michael: Red team strategy, overall competition knowledge
   - Sam: Systems hardening, Windows
   - Tegan: Vulnerability hunting, documentation
   - Others: Whatever their specialty is

3. **Mentor availability**
   - Check schedules (some mentors only available [days])
   - Match to team's meeting times if possible

**Example matching:**

```
Team A (has some experience, just needs accountability):
→ [name] as mentor (1 check-in/week, answer questions)

Team B (mostly new, weak on Windows):
→ [name] as mentor (help with strategy + systems)

Team C (strong team, just wants someone to review work):
→ [name] as mentor (review security docs, give feedback)
```

## Mentor Expectations

**What mentors do:**

| When | What |
|------|------|
| June | "I'm mentoring Team A" (confirm commitment) |
| Sept–Oct | ~1 hr/week (check-in with team, answer questions) |
| Oct–Nov | ~2 hrs/week (more intense during crunch) |
| December | Debrief + feedback |

**What mentors DON'T do:**
- ❌ Do the work for the team (they learn by doing)
- ❌ Show up to every meeting (check-in is enough)
- ❌ Be on the competition team (only if they're listed as participant)
- ❌ Solve problems instantly (help them think through it)

**Mentor approach:**
- Ask questions ("How did you approach this?")
- Guide, not tell ("Have you considered...?")
- Support ("You're doing great, keep going")
- Escalate if needed ("This team is struggling, needs more help")

## Mentor Documentation

**Give each mentor:**

1. **Team roster**
   - Team members' names
   - Their roles + skills
   - Discord channel

2. **Expectations document**
   ```markdown
   # Mentoring Team A
   
   **Your role:** Help with systems hardening + Windows questions
   **Time commitment:** ~1–2 hrs/week
   **Meeting time:** [Team's regular meeting]
   **Check-in:** You can join meetings or do 1-on-1 check-ins (your choice)
   **Escalate if:** Team goes dark for 2+ weeks, anyone says they're overwhelmed
   
   **Contact:** @AJ (admin), @[Team Captain] (team)
   ```

3. **Common questions template**
   - What are the main focus areas for Sept/Oct?
   - What common mistakes should we avoid?
   - How do we know if we're on track?

4. **Resources**
   - Links to `/docs/`, `/resources/`, `/team-formation/`
   - 2024 + 2025 CF team notes
   - CyberForce 101 Library

## Mentor Check-ins

**How often?**
- Once per week minimum (email or Discord message is fine)
- In person if possible (can attend team meeting)
- More during crunch (Oct–Nov)

**What to ask:**
- "How's prep going?"
- "Any blockers I can help with?"
- "How are you feeling about competition?"
- "Need anything from me?"

**If team goes dark:** (no response for 2+ weeks)
- First: Friendly ping ("Haven't heard from you in a bit, checking in!")
- Second: Escalate to AJ ("Team A hasn't responded in 3 weeks, might need intervention")

2. **Share with AJ** for archive + next year's planning

## Mentor Support

**If a mentor is struggling:**

- They can reach out to AJ anytime
- Can hand off to another mentor (no shame in "I'm too busy")
- Can adjust time commitment
- Can escalate ("This team needs more help than I can give")

**You're supporting the mentor too.** Let them know it's okay to ask for help.

---

## Recognition

**Mentors are volunteers giving their time.** Show appreciation:

- Send thank you note after competition
- Mention in year-end summary
- Invite to end-of-year celebration
- Ask them back for next year (they'll likely say yes)

## Troubleshooting

**Q: What if a mentor isn't responsive?**  
A: Reach out ("Haven't heard from you, everything okay?"). If still no response, reassign team to another mentor.

**Q: What if a team doesn't want a mentor?**  
A: That's fine. Offer, but don't force. Some strong teams prefer independence.

**Q: What if a mentor is doing the work for the team?**  
A: Gently remind ("Remember, they need to learn by doing. Your role is guide, not solver").

**Q: What if a mentor has a conflict with the team?**  
A: Reassign if possible. Conflicts happen, no big deal.
