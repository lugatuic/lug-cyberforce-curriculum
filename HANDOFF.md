## Key Success Factors

**Communication**
- Weekly Discord updates (even if just "on track")
- Respond to messages quickly (people get anxious)
- Be honest about what's hard (teams respect reality over false cheeriness)

**Consistency**
- Meetings on the same day/time every week (people plan around it)
- Follow through on commitments ("I'll send that by Friday" → actually send it)
- Show up (if you skip the welcome event, why should they show up?)

**Delegation**
- You don't have to do everything
- Ask others to run technical sessions
- Ask CF/CCDC veterans to give alumni talks
- Ask team captains to own their team's prep
- Your job is to facilitate, not do it all

**Documentation**
- Take notes in every meeting
- Save announcements in `/announcements/`
- Update `/archival/` after competition
- Write handoff notes for next year (this is how the next person survives)

**Mental health**
- Competing in CyberForce/CCDC is stressful
- Your job is to normalize that ("Yes, this is hard, you're not alone")
- Celebrate wins, no matter how small
- Remind people why this matters (learning, skills, team, fun)

## 🚨 Possible Common Pitfalls 

### **Communication**
❌ **Problem:** Some people only provided emails, didn't check Discord  
✅ **Solution:** Use email as primary for official announcements, Discord for engagement

❌ **Problem:** Hard to find resources (scattered across shared drives)  
✅ **Solution:** Everything on this GitHub repo + link from LUG website

### **Team Formation**
❌ **Problem:** People signed up but weren't serious  
✅ **Solution:** Have info session to qualify interest; message confirmation from serious people

❌ **Problem:** Teams may not be balanced (one team had all experts, other had beginners)  
✅ **Solution:** Use skill assessment form (in `/templates/`) to deliberately mix skill levels

### **Windows Systems**
❌ **Problem:** Teams under-prepared on Windows hardening  
✅ **Solution:** Make Windows a priority in September workshops; link resources early

### **Time Management**
❌ **Problem:** C-Suite briefing prep got last-minute  
✅ **Solution:** Start talking about it in October; offer recording slots in advance

❌ **Problem:** Teams went silent for weeks, then panicked  
✅ **Solution:** Check in weekly (not nagging, just "how's it going?"); normalize asking for help

### **Archival**
❌ **Problem:** After competition, nobody remembered key lessons  
✅ **Solution:** Debrief immediately (while fresh); collect scorecards & team reports right away
